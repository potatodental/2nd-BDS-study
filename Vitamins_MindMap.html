B12 vs Folate Deficiency — Critical Distinction

⚠ Exam Trap: Both B12 and Folate deficiency cause Megaloblastic anemia. But ONLY B12 deficiency causes neurological damage (Subacute Combined Degeneration). This is because B12 is also needed for myelin synthesis.
FeatureB12 DeficiencyFolate Deficiency
Megaloblastic anemia✅ YES✅ YES
Neurological damage✅ YES (SCD)❌ NO
Methylmalonic aciduria✅ YES❌ NO
Elevated homocysteine✅ YES✅ YES
Methylfolate trap✅ YES❌ NO
CausesPernicious anemia, vegans, IF deficiencyPregnancy, methotrexate, poor diet

NAD+ vs NADP+ — Critical Distinction

⚠ Key Rule: NADH → enters ETC → generates 2.5 ATP. NADPH → used ONLY for reductive biosynthesis (fatty acids, cholesterol, steroids) — does NOT generate ATP. NADPH is generated mainly by the HMP shunt pathway.
FeatureNAD+ / NADHNADP+ / NADPH
RoleCatabolism / energy generationAnabolic reductive biosynthesis
ATP generationNADH → 2.5 ATP via ETC❌ Does NOT generate ATP
SourceTCA cycle, glycolysisHMP shunt pathway (GPD enzyme)
Key reactionsLactate DH, Pyruvate DH, Isocitrate DHHMG-CoA reductase, FA synthesis

Calcitriol vs Calcitonin

⚠ Frequently Confused! These sound similar but have OPPOSITE effects on blood calcium.
FeatureCalcitriol (Active Vit D)Calcitonin (Thyroid Hormone)
SourceKidney (from Vitamin D)Thyroid parafollicular cells
Effect on Ca++⬆ INCREASES blood calcium⬇ DECREASES blood calcium
MechanismIncreases intestinal absorption, bone resorptionInhibits osteoclasts, promotes osteoblasts
